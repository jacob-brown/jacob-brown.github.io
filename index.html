Experience

Research Project Manager

Kalahari Meerkat Project

I currently hold the position of Project Manager at the Kalahari Meerkat Project, a behavioural and evolutionary biology research field site in the southern tip of the Kalahari desert, South Africa. My role is to facilitate data collection for primarily two research groups, I lead a team of 8-12 research assistants, arranging their working schedule, ensuring their well-being, enthusiasm, and saftey remains high during their stay. Additional responsibilities consist of collecting tissue and morphometric data from, and attaching VHF, GPS, and accelerometer collars to habituated meerkats. I also have experience using cage traps, in which I have caught and collared a number of un-habituated individuals - facilitating tracking of and vists to their groups. Complimentary to the field work, I created a number of R Shiny applications for inserting, handling, and visualising data.

October 2017 - Present
